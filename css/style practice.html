Bhagat Singh

Introduction

Bhagat Singh was one of the greatest freedom fighters of India. He is remembered for his courage, patriotism, and sacrifice for the nation. Born during British rule, he devoted his life to the struggle for India’s independence. Even at a young age, he inspired millions of people with his fearless actions and revolutionary ideas.

Early Life

Bhagat Singh was born on 28 September 1907 in Banga village, Punjab (now in Pakistan). His family was deeply involved in the freedom movement. From childhood, he was influenced by patriotic ideas and stories of brave revolutionaries. He studied at National College in Lahore, where he developed strong nationalist feelings. The tragic Jallianwala Bagh Massacre had a great impact on him. After witnessing the cruelty of British rule, he decided to dedicate his life to freeing India from oppression.

Contribution to Freedom Struggle

Bhagat Singh joined revolutionary organizations and worked with other young patriots. He believed that strong action was necessary to fight against British injustice. Along with his companions, he protested against unfair laws and inspired people to rise against colonial rule. In 1928, he and his fellow revolutionaries sought revenge for the death of Lala Lajpat Rai, who died after a police lathi charge during a protest. Bhagat Singh later became famous for throwing non-lethal bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly along with Batukeshwar Dutt to protest against oppressive laws. Their aim was to “make the deaf hear,” not to harm people.

Ideals and Personality

Bhagat Singh was not only brave but also highly intelligent. He loved reading books and studying political ideas. He believed in equality, freedom, and justice for all people. His slogan “Inquilab Zindabad” became a symbol of revolution and patriotism. He inspired the youth of India to fight against injustice and work for the progress of the country. His dedication and fearless attitude made him a national hero. The British government arrested Bhagat Singh and sentenced him to death. On 23 March 1931, Bhagat Singh, along with Sukhdev Thapar and Shivaram Rajguru, was hanged in Lahore jail. They sacrificed their lives for the freedom of India at a very young age. Their martyrdom shocked the nation and motivated thousands of Indians to join the independence movement.
